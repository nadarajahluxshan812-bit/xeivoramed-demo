XeivoraMed is a trusted-data layer that links a patient's government-verified identity to verified records from certified labs and clinics — so the right information reaches the right provider, with consent, in seconds.

02

A three-way handshake.

Government confirms identity. Certified providers supply the data. The patient grants permission. XeivoraMed connects the three into one verified record.

Identity

Verified by government ID

Identity is confirmed against the national NIC or passport registry — a trusted root no health app can self-report.

Data

Supplied by certified providers

Labs and clinics in the network contribute verified results directly, so every value carries a real, named source.

Permission

Released by patient consent

The patient grants access once. Every pull is logged, scoped and revocable — consent is the key to the whole record.

06

Questions, answered.

Is XeivoraMed live today?+

No — XeivoraMed is at pilot stage. The verification model and provider workflow are built and demonstrable; the data-source integrations are in development and are being piloted with partners. It is not yet for clinical use.

How is the data verified?+

Identity is verified against the national ID registry. Medical values are supplied by certified labs and clinics, and each value is tagged with its source and date. Anything a patient enters themselves is clearly marked as self-reported.

Who controls the patient's data?+

The patient. Access is granted by consent, scoped to a purpose, time-limited, and revocable. Every access is logged immutably, and the patient can see who opened their record, when, and why.

Does a clinic need to change its systems?+

The pilot works through a simple verified-intake step — no rip-and-replace of existing systems. Deeper integration into provider software is part of the later roadmap, not a requirement to start.

Why start in Sri Lanka?+

A mobile-first population, a clear data-protection framework under the PDPA 2022, and a fast-moving private healthcare sector make it a strong first market and a high-fidelity blueprint for other emerging markets.
